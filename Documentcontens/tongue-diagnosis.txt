Tongue Diagnosis in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Tongue examination is a key diagnostic tool in Traditional Chinese Medicine. The tongue reflects the condition of internal organs, Qi, Blood, Yin, and Yang.

Practitioners examine several features of the tongue including color, shape, coating, and moisture.

Tongue Color

Pale Tongue
Often indicates Qi deficiency, Blood deficiency, or Yang deficiency.

Red Tongue
Suggests heat in the body, inflammation, or Yin deficiency.

Purple Tongue
May indicate Blood stagnation or poor circulation.

Tongue Shape

Swollen Tongue
Often related to dampness accumulation or spleen deficiency.

Thin Tongue
May indicate Yin deficiency or blood deficiency.

Tongue Coating

Thin White Coating
Generally considered normal or indicates mild external conditions.

Thick Greasy Coating
Often indicates dampness, phlegm accumulation, or digestive problems.

Yellow Coating
Usually indicates heat or infection.

Peeling or No Coating
Often associated with Yin deficiency or fluid depletion.

Tongue Moisture

Dry Tongue
May indicate fluid deficiency or internal heat.

Wet Tongue
May indicate dampness or cold accumulation.

Clinical Application

Tongue diagnosis is most useful when combined with other diagnostic tools such as pulse examination and symptom analysis.

For example:

A red tongue with little coating may indicate Yin deficiency.

A pale swollen tongue with teeth marks often suggests spleen Qi deficiency.

Tongue diagnosis provides important clues about the overall balance of the body.
