Pulse Diagnosis in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Pulse diagnosis is one of the most important diagnostic techniques in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM). Practitioners evaluate the pulse at the radial artery of the wrist to gather information about the state of the internal organs and the balance of Qi, Blood, Yin, and Yang.

TCM identifies many pulse qualities. Each pulse provides clues about the underlying pattern of imbalance in the body.

Common Pulse Types

Floating Pulse
A floating pulse is felt strongly with light pressure but disappears with deeper pressure. This pulse often indicates exterior conditions such as common cold, wind invasion, or early-stage infections.

Deep Pulse
A deep pulse is only felt when pressing strongly. It typically indicates internal disorders, such as organ dysfunction or chronic disease.

Rapid Pulse
A rapid pulse is faster than normal and often indicates heat in the body. It may occur in fever, inflammation, or emotional agitation.

Slow Pulse
A slow pulse usually indicates cold conditions in the body. It may occur when Yang energy is weak.

Wiry Pulse
A wiry pulse feels tight and like a stretched string. It is commonly associated with Liver Qi stagnation, stress, digestive disorders, or pain.

Slippery Pulse
A slippery pulse feels smooth and flowing like rolling pearls. It often indicates dampness, phlegm accumulation, pregnancy, or food stagnation.

Thin Pulse
A thin pulse feels narrow and weak. It is often associated with Blood deficiency or Yin deficiency.

Choppy Pulse
A choppy pulse feels rough or uneven. It may indicate Blood stagnation or poor circulation.

Clinical Application

Pulse diagnosis is rarely used alone. TCM practitioners combine pulse findings with other diagnostic methods such as tongue examination, symptom inquiry, and observation of the patient’s overall appearance.

For example:

A wiry pulse combined with irritability and rib-side discomfort may indicate Liver Qi stagnation.

A thin pulse with dizziness and pale complexion may suggest Blood deficiency.

Pulse diagnosis requires extensive training and experience to interpret correctly.
