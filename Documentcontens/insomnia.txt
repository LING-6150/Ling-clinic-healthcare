Insomnia in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Insomnia refers to difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking up too early. In TCM, insomnia is often related to imbalance of the Heart, Liver, or Kidney systems.

Common Symptoms

difficulty falling asleep
frequent waking during the night
restless sleep
vivid dreams
daytime fatigue
irritability

Common TCM Patterns

Heart Blood Deficiency
Symptoms include difficulty falling asleep, palpitations, anxiety, and poor memory.

Liver Qi Stagnation
Often caused by stress or emotional tension. Symptoms include irritability, chest tightness, and sleep disturbance.

Heart and Kidney Yin Deficiency
Symptoms include night sweats, anxiety, dry mouth at night, and restlessness.

Phlegm Heat Disturbing the Mind
Symptoms include agitation, heavy head sensation, and mental confusion.

Lifestyle Considerations

Avoid excessive caffeine
Reduce stress
Maintain a regular sleep schedule
Practice relaxation techniques.

Treatment usually involves calming the mind, nourishing blood or Yin, and regulating Qi.
