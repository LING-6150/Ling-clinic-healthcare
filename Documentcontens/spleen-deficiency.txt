Spleen Deficiency in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Spleen deficiency is a common pattern in Traditional Chinese Medicine and is closely related to digestive weakness and low energy.

In TCM theory, the spleen is responsible for transforming food into Qi and Blood. When spleen function becomes weak, digestion and nutrient absorption are impaired.

Common Symptoms

fatigue
poor appetite
abdominal bloating
loose stool
weak muscles
brain fog
low energy after eating

Pathogenesis

Poor diet, excessive worry, chronic illness, or overwork may weaken the spleen system. When spleen Qi becomes deficient, the body cannot properly transform food into energy.

Treatment Principles

Strengthen spleen Qi.
Improve digestive function.
Eliminate dampness accumulation.

Common Herbs

Ren Shen
Dang Shen
Bai Zhu
Fu Ling
Chen Pi

Common Formulas

Si Jun Zi Tang
Liu Jun Zi Tang
Shen Ling Bai Zhu San

Dietary Recommendations

Eat warm and easily digestible foods.
Avoid excessive cold, greasy, or raw foods.
Maintain regular meal times.

Strengthening spleen function is considered essential for maintaining overall energy and health.
