Syndrome Differentiation in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Syndrome differentiation is the central diagnostic principle of Traditional Chinese Medicine. Instead of focusing only on diseases, TCM identifies patterns of imbalance in the body.

A syndrome (pattern) represents a combination of symptoms, tongue signs, pulse qualities, and underlying pathological mechanisms.

Eight Principles of Diagnosis

Exterior vs Interior
Exterior conditions affect the body surface such as skin or muscles. Interior conditions involve internal organs.

Cold vs Heat
Cold patterns include cold limbs, pale tongue, and slow pulse. Heat patterns include fever, thirst, red tongue, and rapid pulse.

Deficiency vs Excess
Deficiency patterns indicate weakness of the body's vital energy. Excess patterns indicate strong pathogenic factors.

Yin vs Yang
Yin represents cold, interior, and deficiency conditions. Yang represents heat, exterior, and excess conditions.

Organ Pattern Differentiation

Liver Pattern
Often related to emotional stress, Qi stagnation, headaches, and menstrual disorders.

Spleen Pattern
Related to digestion, energy production, and dampness accumulation.

Kidney Pattern
Associated with aging, fertility, bones, and chronic weakness.

Heart Pattern
Associated with sleep, mental activity, and emotional balance.

Clinical Importance

Correct syndrome differentiation allows practitioners to choose the most appropriate treatment strategy.

For example, insomnia may result from several patterns including:

Heart blood deficiency
Liver fire rising
Yin deficiency with heat
Phlegm heat disturbing the mind

Each pattern requires a different treatment approach.
