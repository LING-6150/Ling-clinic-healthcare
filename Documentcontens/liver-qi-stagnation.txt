Liver Qi Stagnation in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Liver Qi stagnation is one of the most common patterns diagnosed in Traditional Chinese Medicine. It occurs when the normal flow of Qi in the liver system becomes blocked or constrained.

The liver is responsible for ensuring the smooth circulation of Qi throughout the body. Emotional stress, frustration, and anger are common causes of Liver Qi stagnation.

Common Symptoms

emotional irritability
mood swings
chest or rib-side discomfort
frequent sighing
digestive problems
irregular menstruation
headache related to stress

Pathogenesis

Emotional stress disrupts the free flow of Qi in the liver system. When Qi becomes stagnant, it may affect other organs such as the spleen and stomach, leading to digestive problems or fatigue.

Treatment Principles

The primary treatment principle is to soothe the liver and regulate Qi circulation.

Common Herbs

Chai Hu
Xiang Fu
Bai Shao
Chen Pi

Common Formula

Xiao Yao San

Clinical Application

Treatment aims to restore smooth Qi flow, reduce emotional tension, and support digestive function.

Lifestyle Recommendations

Reduce emotional stress.
Practice relaxation techniques.
Maintain regular physical activity to promote Qi circulation.
