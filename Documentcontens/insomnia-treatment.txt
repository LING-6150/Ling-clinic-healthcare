Treatment of Insomnia in Traditional Chinese Medicine

Insomnia is a common condition in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM). It is usually related to imbalance in the Heart, Liver, or Kidney systems. Treatment focuses on calming the mind, nourishing blood, and restoring the balance of Yin and Yang.

Common Treatment Principles

1. Nourish Heart Blood
Heart blood deficiency may cause difficulty falling asleep, palpitations, anxiety, and poor memory. Treatment focuses on nourishing blood and calming the spirit.

Common herbs:
Dang Gui
Bai Shao
Suan Zao Ren
Long Yan Rou

Common formula:
Suan Zao Ren Tang

2. Calm the Spirit

When emotional stress disturbs sleep, the mind becomes restless. Herbs that calm the spirit help stabilize mental activity and promote sleep.

Common herbs:
Suan Zao Ren
He Huan Pi
Fu Shen
Yuan Zhi

3. Nourish Yin and Clear Heat

In some patients, insomnia is caused by Yin deficiency leading to internal heat. Symptoms may include night sweats, irritability, and dry mouth at night.

Treatment principle:
nourish Yin and clear heat.

Common formula:
Tian Wang Bu Xin Dan

4. Regulate Liver Qi

Emotional stress may cause Liver Qi stagnation, which interferes with sleep. Treatment focuses on regulating Qi circulation and relieving emotional tension.

Common formula:
Xiao Yao San

Lifestyle Recommendations

Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
Reduce stress and emotional tension.
Avoid caffeine and stimulating activities before bedtime.

Proper treatment aims to restore harmony between the Heart and Kidney systems and calm the spirit.
