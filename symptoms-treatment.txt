Insomnia in Traditional Chinese Medicine is classified into several patterns. Heart and kidney disharmony presents with difficulty falling asleep, palpitations, night sweats, and lower back weakness. Treatment focuses on nourishing heart Yin and tonifying kidney essence using herbs like Suan Zao Ren, Bai Zi Ren, and Nu Zhen Zi. Heart and spleen deficiency presents with excessive dreaming, waking easily, poor memory, and fatigue. Treatment tonifies heart blood and spleen Qi using herbs like Dang Gui, Long Yan Rou, and Huang Qi. Liver fire disturbing the heart presents with difficulty falling asleep, irritability, headache, and bitter taste. Treatment clears liver fire and calms the mind using Huang Lian, Zhi Zi, and Long Dan Cao.

Digestive disorders in TCM are primarily related to spleen and stomach dysfunction. Spleen Qi deficiency presents with poor appetite, bloating after meals, loose stools, and fatigue. Treatment strengthens spleen Qi using Bai Zhu, Fu Ling, Dang Shen, and Gan Cao. Stomach Yin deficiency presents with dry mouth, hunger without appetite, and constipation. Treatment nourishes stomach Yin using Mai Men Dong, Shi Hu, and Yu Zhu. Food stagnation presents with abdominal bloating, belching, acid reflux, and foul breath. Treatment promotes digestion using Shan Zha, Shen Qu, and Mai Ya.

Headache in TCM has multiple patterns. Wind cold headache presents with occipital pain, stiff neck, and aversion to cold. Treatment expels wind cold using Chuan Xiong, Bai Zhi, and Fang Feng. Liver Yang rising headache presents with throbbing temporal pain, dizziness, irritability, and red eyes. Treatment calms liver Yang using Tian Ma, Gou Teng, and Shi Jue Ming. Qi and blood deficiency headache presents with dull persistent pain that worsens with fatigue. Treatment tonifies Qi and blood using Huang Qi, Dang Gui, and Chuan Xiong.

Fatigue and chronic tiredness in TCM is usually related to Qi deficiency or Yang deficiency. Qi deficiency fatigue presents with shortness of breath, low energy, spontaneous sweating, and pale complexion. Treatment tonifies Qi using Ren Shen, Huang Qi, and Bai Zhu. Yang deficiency fatigue presents with cold limbs, lower back weakness, frequent urination, and lack of motivation. Treatment warms Yang using Fu Zi, Rou Gui, and Du Zhong.

Anxiety and emotional disorders in TCM are often related to liver Qi stagnation or heart fire. Liver Qi stagnation presents with chest tightness, sighing, irritability, and mood swings. Treatment spreads liver Qi using Chai Hu, Bai Shao, and Xiang Fu. Heart fire presents with anxiety, palpitations, mouth sores, and insomnia. Treatment clears heart fire using Huang Lian, Lian Zi Xin, and Zhu Sha.

Lower back pain in TCM is related to kidney deficiency or external pathogenic invasion. Kidney Yang deficiency lower back pain presents with dull aching pain, cold sensations, and worse in the morning. Treatment warms kidney Yang using Du Zhong, Bu Gu Zhi, and Xu Duan. Kidney Yin deficiency lower back pain presents with burning sensation, dryness, and worse at night. Treatment nourishes kidney Yin using Shu Di Huang, Gou Qi Zi, and Nu Zhen Zi.
