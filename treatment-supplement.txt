1. Core Treatment Principles in TCM
-------------------------------------------------

Traditional Chinese Medicine treatment focuses on restoring balance in the body rather than targeting a single symptom. Treatment strategies are based on pattern differentiation.

Key treatment principles include:

1. Tonify deficiency
2. Clear excess
3. Regulate Qi circulation
4. Nourish blood
5. Balance Yin and Yang
6. Eliminate pathogenic factors such as dampness, phlegm, heat, or cold
7. Strengthen organ systems such as the spleen, liver, kidney, and lung.

Treatment methods often combine herbal formulas, acupuncture, diet therapy, lifestyle adjustments, and stress management.

-------------------------------------------------
2. Spleen and Digestive Disorders
-------------------------------------------------

Digestive disorders are extremely common in TCM clinical practice. The spleen and stomach are considered the central organs of digestion and energy production.

Common symptoms:
bloating, poor appetite, loose stool, fatigue, abdominal distention.

Pattern: Spleen Qi Deficiency

Treatment principle:
tonify spleen Qi and improve digestion.

Common herbal formula:
Si Jun Zi Tang (Four Gentlemen Decoction)

Key herbs:
Ginseng
Atractylodes
Poria
Licorice

Expected outcomes:
improved digestion
better energy levels
reduced bloating.

---

Pattern: Spleen Dampness Accumulation

Symptoms:
heaviness in body
sticky stool
brain fog
fatigue
thick tongue coating.

Treatment principle:
dry dampness and strengthen spleen.

Common formula:
Ping Wei San.

Key herbs:
Atractylodes
Magnolia Bark
Chen Pi
Licorice.

---

Pattern: Food Stagnation

Symptoms:
abdominal fullness after eating
belching
acid reflux
bad breath.

Treatment principle:
promote digestion and move food stagnation.

Common formula:
Bao He Wan.

3. Qi and Blood Deficiency
-------------------------------------------------

Qi and Blood deficiency is often related to chronic fatigue, dizziness, pale complexion, and weakness.

Pattern: Qi Deficiency

Symptoms:
fatigue
shortness of breath
weak voice
spontaneous sweating.

Treatment principle:
tonify Qi.

Common formula:
Bu Zhong Yi Qi Tang.

Key herbs:
Astragalus
Ginseng
Atractylodes
Angelica Sinensis.

---

Pattern: Blood Deficiency

Symptoms:
pale complexion
dry skin
dizziness
insomnia
scanty menstruation.

Treatment principle:
nourish blood.

Common formula:
Si Wu Tang.

Key herbs:
Angelica Sinensis
Rehmannia
White Peony
Ligusticum.

---

Pattern: Qi and Blood Deficiency Combined

Treatment principle:
tonify Qi and nourish blood.

Classic formula:
Ba Zhen Tang.

-------------------------------------------------

Chronic fatigue often involves multiple organ systems.

Possible patterns:

You Gui Wan

6. Female Fertility and Reproductive Health
-------------------------------------------------

In TCM, fertility is closely related to kidney essence, liver blood, and spleen function.

Common causes of infertility patterns:

Kidney deficiency
Liver Qi stagnation
Blood stasis
Dampness obstruction.

Pattern: Kidney Essence Deficiency

Symptoms:
irregular periods
low back pain
fatigue
difficulty conceiving.

Treatment principle:
tonify kidney essence.

Formula:
Zuo Gui Wan or You Gui Wan.

---

Pattern: Liver Qi Stagnation affecting reproduction

Symptoms:
PMS
irregular cycles
emotional stress.

Treatment principle:
regulate liver Qi.

Formula:
Xiao Yao San.

---

Pattern: Blood Stasis in uterus

Symptoms:
painful periods
dark clots
sharp pelvic pain.

Treatment principle:
invigorate blood circulation.

Formula:
Gui Zhi Fu Ling Wan.

---

Pattern: Spleen deficiency affecting fertility

fatigue

Treatment principle:
strengthen spleen and nourish blood.

Formula:
Ba Zhen Tang.

-------------------------------------------------
10. Lifestyle and Dietary Therapy
-------------------------------------------------

Treatment is not limited to herbal medicine.

Key lifestyle recommendations include:

balanced diet
regular sleep schedule
moderate exercise
stress reduction
avoiding excessive cold or greasy foods.

Digestive health is especially important since the spleen and stomach generate Qi and blood.

-------------------------------------------------
11. Integrative Perspective
-------------------------------------------------

Modern TCM practitioners often combine traditional approaches with biomedical evaluation.

For persistent or severe symptoms, medical evaluation and laboratory testing are recommended.

TCM treatment works best when combined with healthy lifestyle habits and appropriate medical care.
